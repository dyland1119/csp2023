4.1.4 Project

In this project RJ and I selected a simulation to observe and study from NetLogo. We decided on the Lighting simulation. This model showed how lightning works and how the negative ions from the clouds connect with the dust and trees on the ground. We could change the size of the cloud, its strength, the number of trees, and the amount of dust. We observed the different changes that happened when each parameter was changed.

3.1.6 Project

This is the 3.1.6 project in which Mark, Luke, and I organized the messy data set into four separate data sets. After we turned the data into graphs, we figured out that the data sets were from the Rocky Mountains environment in the Alien Planet. In the rocky mountains, there are low temperatures that can heat up quickly, bright light that can be occasionally interrupted by clouds, and a low and constant flow of wind that gives off a steady sound level. After reviewing the correct answer, we were wrong in our assumption. We believed it to be the Rocky Mountain biome, but it was actually the Island Forrest biome. The part we were incorrect in was our labeling of the graphs. We has the temperature and light graphs switched and that impacted our decision greatly.
